# Original Concept Reference

Source: Matt's original Daily Call concept site.

## Positioning

Daily Call provides an affordable daily phone check-in service for elderly or disabled people who live alone.

The original model used trusted friendly human Care Callers. The new product direction keeps the same promise but uses an AI voice caller to make the service easier to scale.

## Core service promise

- Call every day at a regular scheduled time.
- Keep calls brief, approximately 1 minute.
- Say hello and check in warmly.
- Ask about immediate health or safety concerns.
- Send daily check-in reports to next of kin via text message.
- Send emergency/urgent notifications when concerning information is detected.

## Original pricing reference

Historical rates from original concept:

- Setup fee: $15 one time.
- 1 call/day, 7 days/week: $34.95/month.
- 2 calls/day, 7 days/week: $64.95/month.
- 3 calls/day, 7 days/week: $89.95/month.
- Building/complex owner discounts for resident amenities.

These are reference points, not final pricing decisions.

## Disclaimer posture

Dailycall should clearly state:

- It is not a medical service.
- Callers/AI are not medical practitioners, registered nurses, or social workers.
- It does not diagnose, treat, or report physical/mental health issues as a clinical authority.
- It does not replace home care services from registered healthcare professionals.
- It can report concerning information to family/caregivers/proper contacts according to configured rules.

## Product translation to AI version

Original human Care Caller language should become AI-safe language:

- “trusted friendly Care Callers” → “friendly AI voice check-ins supervised by service rules/operator controls.”
- “assess and report” → “identify configured concern signals and notify caregivers.”
- “emergency notifications” → “urgent caregiver/next-of-kin alerts,” unless actual emergency services integration exists.
